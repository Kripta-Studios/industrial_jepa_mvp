Surface quality

Machine drift

Too many signals

How to say it at Audi

Use a simple factory story.

The story

“Imagine a line where every part image, every torque curve and every sensor trace becomes one ranked review queue. The system does not replace your quality engineers. It tells them where to look first.”

The ask

“Give us one process, one defect/risk objective and a small historical dataset. In 4-6 weeks we show whether this can reduce inspection burden, false alarms or late defect detection.”

Pilot plan

4-6 weeks, one concrete line or process.

Week 1

Data audit: images, sensors, logs, labels, failure/quality events.

Week 2

Baselines: simple rules, visual foundation, sensor/process baseline.

Weeks 3-4

Modeling: heatmaps, risk scores, alert ranking, calibration.

Weeks 5-6

Review: metrics, examples, business case and integration decision.

What we need

Ask for a small, concrete dataset.

Minimum data

100-1,000 images or historical sensor windows from one process, plus timestamps and any known quality/failure labels.

Best data

Images + sensor traces + process settings + line/station IDs + inspection result + maintenance or quality event history.

The first pilot should be narrow: one product family, one line, one quality/risk question.

What the client receives

A decision package, not just a model.

Demo

Local HTML/PDF showing examples, heatmaps, scores and ranked alerts.

Metrics

AUPRC/AUROC, Precision@10, false alarm rate, lead time when applicable.

Go / no-go

Clear recommendation: integrate, refine, change data collection or stop.

What not to say

Keep the pitch confident, but credible.

Say this

“We can run a fast pilot to prove whether your images, sensors and process logs can predict quality/risk earlier than current rules.”

Do not say this

“This is guaranteed to work in production immediately” or “we replace your engineers.” The offer is a rigorous pilot with practical outputs.
