# Spain Industrial Outreach Playbook

EVOCON Solutions - Industrial Predictive Quality MVP

This document is written for a non-technical seller preparing outreach to Audi and other Spanish industrial companies. It is deliberately practical: who to contact, what to sell, what to send, and what to say.

## 1. The Core Sales Message

Use this sentence first:

> EVOCON helps factories turn images, sensors and process logs into earlier warnings, defect heatmaps and a ranked list of what to inspect first.

Use this sentence to explain the pilot:

> We are not asking you to replace your production systems. We propose a narrow 4-6 week pilot on one line or process, using your historical data, to prove whether we can detect quality or machine-risk issues earlier than current checks.

Use this sentence to explain why EVOCON is credible:

> EVOCON already works in industrial automation, PLC/robotics support and production-project execution; this MVP adds an AI quality layer on top of existing factory data.

## 2. What To Sell

Sell a pilot, not a platform.

The pilot has three modules:

| Module | Simple explanation | Best first buyers |
|---|---|---|
| Visual defect detection | The system marks suspicious zones on a product image and ranks which parts need review. | Automotive, electronics, wood, packaging, food, aerospace |
| Machine risk scoring | The system uses vibration, current, temperature, torque, SCADA or test logs to rank cycles/assets by risk. | Automotive, energy, shipbuilding, process industry |
| Process deviation detection | The system learns normal process evolution and highlights when reality starts drifting. | Automotive, pharma, energy, food, bioprocess, telemetry |

Do not lead with model names. DINOv2, PatchCore, PaDiM, JEPA and world models are technical proof points, not the first sales message.

### First question to ask Audi

> Where do you currently detect defects, machine drift or station anomalies too late?

### If Audi says "visual defects"

> Then the first pilot should be visual. We take normal and suspect part images, produce heatmaps over the suspicious zones, and rank parts for review. The output is not a vague AI score; it is a review queue and a visual explanation.

### If Audi says "machine downtime"

> Then the first pilot should use machine and process signals: vibration, current, torque, temperature, PLC/SCADA values or test logs. We rank cycles, tools or assets by risk and measure whether alerts arrive earlier with fewer false alarms.

### If Audi says "we already have systems"

> That is good. The pilot does not replace them. It benchmarks whether an extra layer can reduce late detection or false alarms. If it does not beat your current baseline, the pilot gives you a clear no-go decision.

## 8. Phone Call Script

Start:

> We are not selling a generic AI dashboard. We are proposing one narrow pilot to see whether existing factory data can reduce late detection or false alarms.

Ask:

1. What defect or machine issue is usually detected too late?
2. What data already exists: images, sensors, PLC/SCADA, test logs, maintenance events?
3. How do you currently decide what to inspect first?
4. What would make a pilot valuable: fewer false alarms, earlier warning, better heatmaps, or less manual inspection?
5. Who owns the data and who can validate false positives?

Close:

> The next step is not a big project. It is a 30-minute technical scoping call with one process owner and one data owner.

## 9. What Not To Say

Avoid:

- "This is the best model."
- "This guarantees zero defects."
- "This is ready for full production."
- "The model is autonomous."
- "We already know it will beat your current system."
- "JEPA/DINO/PatchCore" as the first sentence.

Use:

- "pilot-ready MVP";
- "ranked review queue";
- "heatmaps and early warning";
- "compare against your current baseline";
- "clear go/no-go result";
- "uses existing plant data."
